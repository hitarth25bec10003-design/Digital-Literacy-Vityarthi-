TASK 5 – Cybercrime Awareness 

	CASE STUDY ( Identity Theft )

Identity theft is a cybercrime where someone steals another person’s personal information and uses it for financial gain or fraudulent activities. This information may include Aadhaar number, bank details, passwords, or college ID information.

A realistic example involves a college student named Rahul. He received an email claiming to be from his university asking him to “verify his student account” to continue accessing online classes. The email looked official and included the college logo.

The crime unfolded as follows:
First, Rahul clicked the link in the email.
Second, he was redirected to a fake website that looked identical to his college portal.
Third, he entered his login ID, password, phone number, and bank-linked UPI details.
Fourth, the cybercriminals collected this information instantly.
Finally, within hours, Rahul noticed unauthorized transactions from his bank account and suspicious emails sent from his account.

College students are often targeted because they frequently use online platforms, may lack cybersecurity awareness, and often share personal details online. Students applying for internships, scholarships, or part-time jobs are especially vulnerable.

The consequences of identity theft can be serious. Victims may lose money, face unauthorized loans, experience damage to their credit score, and suffer emotional stress. Additionally, recovering stolen identity information can take weeks or months.

This case highlights the importance of verifying emails, avoiding suspicious links, and protecting personal information online.
